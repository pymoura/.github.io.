Sage Meals  

Meal Planning Made Easy

Sage Meals is a mobile application that helps people plan meals easily. This is our project (UX Case Study) for a UX and Interaction class at UCI. 

Team: Jing Gu, Yuxin Huang, Paula Yamashita de Moura

Duration: 10 weeks

Tools: Figma, Canva, Miro, Google Docs

Project Description

Problem Statement

How can we help people with limited free time to plan their meals around the individual preferences and guidelines that work flexibly with their schedules?

Initially, we were working on a solution for busy people who want to plan their meals, so they can follow a diet. As our  research evolved, the problem we were focusing on changed. We listened to our users, and we understood that people need to think about different things when they are planning their meals, especially about dealing with different dietary preferences between family members and the time spent on cooking. 

“It can get complicated, sometimes I make one thing that my daughter will eat and another thing that I will eat, and sometimes a third item that is going to be my mother's food.”

“I spend too much time on cooking, if I spend less time with it, I would have more time with my son, playing or doing enjoyable things.” 

Solution

Sage Meals is an app that can help people to plan their meals easily and based on their family preferences. Users can add their family members dietary preferences and restrictions, and the app will suggest meals that will fit all individuals needs.

Users Interviews

We interviewed 9 people between 24 to 65 years old, men and women, who have limited free time due to work, study, or family responsibilities and were the person in charge of cooking.

Design Communication

Persona

We created our primary persona, Lily White, to represent most of our interviewees, who expressed their major needs and shared similar expectations, goals, attitudes, and behaviors. The persona helped us to figure out a quick and inexpensive way to prioritize the features throughout the development process. From the persona of Lily White, it is possible to see that our target users are busy with their work/ school schedule, are responsible to cook for the family, and wish to spend less time in the kitchen, but want to cook delicious food. 

Journey Map

We create a user journey map of Lily White’s weekly meal planning. It maps out her feelings, actions, and the tasks she experiences when preparing and executing her meal plan. The Lilly White journey map represents most of what we heard from the users we interviewed, what tasks they need to perform, their expectations, and emotions during their journey of planning meals and cooking for the whole family. 

Storyboard

We created this storyboard to tell the story of our persona (a full-time worker and mother) that struggles to plan the family meals. The character Lilly gets anxious and tired of trying to find a meal that fits all individual’s needs (dietary preferences, allergies, or picky eater children), which seems to be a common issue among our target users. The main point of this storyboard was to depict the struggle that planning meals can be, and how we can help people with this task, resulting in people being happy and able to enjoy their free time on other things.
