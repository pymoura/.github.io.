Sage Meals  

Meal Planning Made Easy

Sage Meals is a mobile application that helps people plan meals easily. This is our project (UX Case Study) for a UX and Interaction class at UCI. 

Team: Jing Gu, Yuxin Huang, Paula Yamashita de Moura

Duration: 10 weeks

Tools: Figma, Canva, Miro, Google Docs

Project Description

Problem Statement

How can we help people with limited free time to plan their meals around the individual preferences and guidelines that work flexibly with their schedules?

Initially, we were working on a solution for busy people who want to plan their meals, so they can follow a diet. As our  research evolved, the problem we were focusing on changed. We listened to our users, and we understood that people need to think about different things when they are planning their meals, especially about dealing with different dietary preferences between family members and the time spent on cooking. 

“It can get complicated, sometimes I make one thing that my daughter will eat and another thing that I will eat, and sometimes a third item that is going to be my mother's food.”

“I spend too much time on cooking, if I spend less time with it, I would have more time with my son, playing or doing enjoyable things.” 

Solution

Sage Meals helps people to plan their meals easily, so they can save time and prepare delicious meals for their families. Users can add their family members and their individual dietary preferences and restrictions, and the app will recommend recipes based on people's dietary preferences and restrictions, so people can customize their meal plans that work flexibly with their schedule and lifestyle. 

Users Interviews

We interviewed 9 people between 24 to 60 years old, men and women, who have limited free time due to work, study, or family responsibilities and were the person in charge of cooking.

  • 6 people cook for their family (3 have small children), and 3 cooks for themselves;
  • 5 people plan their meals by writing it down or recording it in their mind. Some use meal subscription services, and 4 people do not plan their meals ahead of time;
  • 5 people said they spent too much time cooking. They wish to spend less time at kitchen, and  use the time to do more enjoyable things;
  • 4 people said they enjoy cooking;
  • 4 people think they are beginner cooks, therefore they would end up spending too much time on cooking;
  • 4 people think they are beginner cooks and 3 people think they cook very well;

Design Communication

Persona

We created our primary persona, Lily White, to represent most of our interviewees, who expressed their major needs and shared similar expectations, goals, attitudes, and behaviors. The persona helped us to figure out a quick and inexpensive way to prioritize the features throughout the development process. From the persona of Lily White, it is possible to see that our target users are busy with their work/ school schedule, are responsible to cook for the family, and wish to spend less time in the kitchen, but want to cook delicious food. 

Journey Map

We create a user journey map of Lily White’s weekly meal planning. It maps out her feelings, actions, and the tasks she experiences when preparing and executing her meal plan. The Lilly White journey map represents most of what we heard from the users we interviewed, what tasks they need to perform, their expectations, and emotions during their journey of planning meals and cooking for the whole family. 

Storyboard

We created this storyboard to tell the story of our persona (a full-time worker and mother) that struggles to plan the family meals. The character Lilly gets anxious and tired of trying to find a meal that fits all individual’s needs (dietary preferences, allergies, or picky eater children), which seems to be a common issue among our target users. The main point of this storyboard was to depict the struggle that planning meals can be, and how we can help people with this task, resulting in people being happy and able to enjoy their free time on other things.

Wireframe

We created a low-fidelity prototype for our application. We used the digital tool, Figma, to create the wireframes. This helped us to mock the user experience of our application.  From these wireframes we were able to simulate the desires of people with limited free time, who want to cook healthy and delicious meals for their family. Users would receive meal recommendations that match with their desired time for cooking, effortlessly find different recipes to meet all of their family individual needs, receive recipes recommendations for their meal plan based on the ingredients they have available at their home, and automatically generate a shopping list based on the recipes they added to their meal plan.

    Reflections & Next Steps

    The process of creating Sage Meals was an interesting and challenging activity. We not only learned models, techniques, and methods to design and develop an application, but also understood how crucial it is to keep the focus on the users needs when developing a product. Moreover, from this experience we learned a couple important lessons:

    • Nothing is set in stone. During the development process we will learn things from our users that we did not think of, therefore things may change, and we may end up with a completely different product.
    • Users always come first. We need to listen to what the users want. We are designing a product for them, not for ourselves. 
    • Evaluation and testing are crucial to find usability flaws and to highlight the strengths of prototypes.
    • Designing a product (application) is not a straight development - with starting and ending points, it is an iterative process, a continually improving approach. 

    As a result of this project, we have a low-fidelity prototype that gives us a good idea how the application could work. There are a couple more iterations we still need to work on, so it gets close to a real world application. For next steps we need to:

    • Refine the prototype based on the evaluation findings
    • Do usability testing, user testing, and iterate on the prototype to evolve it to high fidelity prototype.
